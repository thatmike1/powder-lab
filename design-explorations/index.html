07
Pixel Workbench
A loving pixel-art paint tool (Aseprite / Deluxe Paint). Warm-dark chrome, hand-pixeled icons, textured material tiles, the selected material's color as the accent.
08
Fantasy Console
A PICO-8 handheld. The full-color sim glows inside a chunky screen bezel; the chrome commits to a tiny fixed console palette. Boldest and most characterful.
09
In-World (Noita)
Chrome carved from the cave the sim lives in. Dithered-noise stone, carved pixel bevels, firelight glow built from hard dither rings. Moodiest, most "game".

Round 1 — missed the brief

Web styles bolted onto a pixel toy. Kept for reference / contrast, but none of these respected the medium.

01
Lab Instrument
Clinical and scientific. Monospace readouts, calibration ticks, gridded panels, restrained cyan + amber data accents.
02
Refined Gallery
The quietest. Deep neutral, generous space, type-led hierarchy, chrome nearly invisible, the sim framed like art.
03
Arcade Neon
The loudest. The canvas is a glowing screen, swatches lit with their material color, chunky game-HUD controls, saturated bloom.
04
Retro CRT
Phosphor terminal. Near-black with green/amber glow, scanlines, all-monospace, pixel-era bezel. Coherent with the pixels.
05
Brutalist Editorial
Stark and confident. Off-white + ink, hard 1px rules, exposed grid, grotesk + mono type, one hot accent. The anti-SaaS.
06
Risograph Print
Warm paper, two or three spot inks, halftone grain. Playful and sophisticated without being childish. Screenshot-friendly.
